My project is focused on helping develop TraSHDUMP (TRAnsits with any Stellar Host Detected with an Unbiased Matched-filter Pipeline) and RECYCLEBin (Removal of Exoplanet Candidates Yearning to be Condoned as Lookalikes and Eclipsing BINaries), automated pipelines for the detection and vetting of exoplanet candidates, respectively. This project is set to significantly expand the Kepler transiting planet sample, leading to more precise and complete occurrence rate estimates for small planets, and can additionally be applied to TESS, K2, and Plato.

Accretion and Wind Variability of T Tauri Stars Diagnosed through H-alpha Variations

During summer 2024 and my senior year at Rice University, I worked with Dr. Chris Johns-Krull for my senior thesis. I explored the variability in Classical T Tauri Stars (CTTSs) by analyzing high-resolution spectroscopic data. Specifically, I studied the H-alpha emission line at 6562.817 Angstroms, which serves as a tool to examine the relationship between accretion and wind processes. The location of the launching mechanisms for these winds is a subject of significant debate in literature. To analyze this variability, I applied several techniques aimed at identifying and characterizing variability and their origins, such as periodograms, Principal Component Analysis, and Correlation Matrices. I presented my work as a poster at the Gulf Coast Research Symposium and at "The Role of Accretion and Ejection Variability in the Evolution of Young Stars and their Disks" (RAVEYSO) Workshop. I am currently working on adding the Forbidden Oxygen emission line at 6300 Angstroms to further analyze the variability of two CTTSs BP Tau and CI Tau. A paper on my results is currently in prep.

Localizing Stellar Activity on Low-Mass Stars with the Transiting Exoplanet Survey Satellite

During summer 2023, I was an intern at Goddard Space Flight Center working with Dr. Josh Schlieder and Jordan Ealy. My project was on localizing stellar activity on low-mass stars using TESS 120s SPOC calibrated data. I created and cleaned my sample of light curves using Lightkurve and localized flares using AltaiPony. My clean sample consisted of K and M stars from five nearby moving groups: Beta Pictoris, AB Doradus, Tucana-Horologium, Columba, and Carina, spanning ages ~ 20 to 150 Myr. I presented my findings on the relationship between spot modulation and flare activity as a poster at the Conference for Undergraduate Women in Physics, 243rd Meeting of the American Astronomical Society, and Cool Stars 22. I continued to work on my research after my internship until I published my findings in Research Notes of the AAS in May 2024. (DOI 10.3847/2515-5172/ad4da0).
